What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

Tan uses two quotes:
"Self-awareness is a neutral mode that maintains self-reflectiveness even in the midst of turbulent emotions." -Daniel Goleman
"Mindfulness means paying attention in a particular way: on purpose, in the present moment, and non-judgmentally." -Jon Kabat-Zinn
Basically both concepts exist for the same purpose - being mindful and self aware is to pay attention to yourself and your emotions so as not to let them become or consume you.

Try at least one of the self awareness practices from the chapter (Body Scan, Journaling). What was your experience of it? How did you feel?

Because I already keep a journal I decided to try the body scan technique. Initially I found it difficult to concentrate because the ambience at my place/krd isn't exactly relaxed. However I took my time to really focus and found it to be very soothing. I'm currently quite emotionally exhausted and it was amazing to be able to let everything go for a moment and actually feel it leave my body.

What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

Honeslty I found that part of the chapter so important and definitely something I needed to read. I think it's so easy to forget that our emotions aren't our core selves-that they come and go and we experience them only momentarily. Though I also think it's important to remember that people suffer from chronic depression and anxiety as well as many other forms of mental illness it can take a lot more than being mindful and self aware for them to overcome and deal with it.
